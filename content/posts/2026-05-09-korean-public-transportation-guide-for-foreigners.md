South Korea boasts one of the world's most efficient and user-friendly public transportation systems. For foreigners living in or visiting Korea, mastering the subway, bus networks, and the ubiquitous T-money card can greatly enhance mobility and save time. This guide provides detailed, practical information on how to navigate Korea's public transit, including fare payment, etiquette, and recommended apps.

## Overview of Korea's Public Transportation

Korea's public transportation integrates subway, bus, and rail services across major cities like Seoul, Busan, Daegu, and Incheon. The system is known for its punctuality, cleanliness, and extensive coverage. Foreigners can use a single rechargeable card, T-money, for almost all forms of transit, including subways, buses, and even some taxis and convenience stores.

### Key Features

- **Integration**: Subway, bus, and some rail services accept T-money.
- **Affordability**: Base fares are low, with discounts for transfers.
- **Convenience**: Real-time arrival information is available via apps and digital signs at stations.
- **Accessibility**: Stations feature elevators, escalators, and priority seating for elderly and disabled passengers.
- **Safety**: Korea's transit is consistently ranked among the safest in the world, with low crime rates and helpful staff.

## Using the Subway

The subway is the backbone of urban transit in Seoul and other metropolitan areas. It is fast, reliable, and connects major districts, tourist attractions, and business centers.

### Lines and Stations

Seoul Metro operates lines numbered 1 through 9, plus the Shinbundang Line, AREX (airport railroad), and Gyeongui-Jungang Line. Each line is color-coded and station signs are bilingual in Korean and English. In addition, Korail operates commuter rail lines that connect suburban areas to the city center.

### Fare Structure

As of 2026, the basic subway fare for adults using T-money is approximately 1,400 won for distances up to 10 km. Additional distance charges apply per 5 km. Transfer discounts allow up to four transfers within 30 minutes for an additional 100 won per transfer. There are also discounted fares for children, teenagers, and seniors with proper identification.

### How to Ride

1. **Purchase or Reload T-money**: Available at convenience stores (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven, Ministop) and station vending machines.
2. **Tap In**: Place your card on the reader at the turnstile when entering and exiting.
3. **Follow Signs**: Follow overhead signs to your destination line and platform.
4. **Mind the Gap**: Watch for the gap between train and platform.
5. **Etiquette**: Offer seats to elderly, pregnant, disabled, and passengers with small children. Avoid eating or drinking on trains.
6. **Noise**: Keep conversations at a moderate volume and avoid loud phone calls.

### Apps for Subway Navigation

- **Subway Korea**: Provides real-time arrival, route planning, and station maps.
- **KakaoMap** or **Naver Map**: Offer comprehensive transit directions including subway and bus transfers.
- **Google Maps**: Also reliable for transit directions in Korea.
- **Naver Transit**: Specialized for subway and bus routes with live updates.

## Using the Bus

Buses complement the subway by reaching areas not directly served by rail. Seoul alone has thousands of bus routes, including city buses, village buses, and airport limousines.

### Types of Buses

- **City Buses (시내버스)**: Operate within city limits, numbered with three digits.
- **Village Buses (마을버스)**: Serve residential neighborhoods, often with two-digit numbers.
- **Express Buses (광역버스)**: Connect satellite cities to Seoul, often with higher fares.
- **Airport Limousine Buses**: Direct service between Incheon/Gimpo Airports and major hotels or districts.
- **Shuttle Buses**: Free or low-cost buses operated by hotels, shopping malls, and theme parks.

### Fare Structure

City bus fares are similar to subway: 1,400 won for adults with T-money, with distance-based increments. Transfer discounts apply when switching between bus and subway within 30 minutes. Some premium buses (like airport limousines) have flat rates based on route.

### How to Ride

1. **Check the Route**: Look at the electronic sign at the bus stop or use an app to confirm the bus number and destination.
2. **Board and Tap In**: Enter through the front door, tap your T-money on the reader near the driver.
3. **Alight and Tap Out**: For buses that require tap-out (most city buses), tap again on the reader near the rear door before exiting. If you forget, you may be charged the maximum fare.
4. **Etiquette**: Offer priority seats, avoid loud conversations, and keep luggage off seats.
5. **Boarding**: Allow passengers to exit before boarding; move to the rear of the bus if possible to expedite boarding.

### Apps for Bus Navigation

- **Bisangse (Bus Arrival)**: Provides real-time bus arrival times at stops.
- **Seoul Bus**: Official app from Seoul Metropolitan Government with route maps and arrival info.
- **KakaoT**: Includes taxi-hailing but also bus route planning.
- **Naver Map**: Comprehensive transit directions with live bus locations.

## The T-money Card

T-money is a rechargeable smart card used for transit, retail, and other services. It is the most convenient way to pay for public transportation in Korea.

### Where to Get and Reload

- **Convenience Stores**: GS25, CU, 7-Eleven, Ministop.
- **Station Vending Machines**: Located at most subway stations.
- **T-money Official Website**: For online balance check and reload (requires Korean bank account).
- **Bus Ticketing Machines**: Some buses have machines where you can reload T-money.

### Benefits

- **Transfer Discounts**: Automatic application of discounts when transferring between subway and bus.
- **Balance Protection**: Can be registered online to protect against loss.
- **Multi-use**: Accepted at many convenience stores, pharmacies, and even some taxis.
- **Offline Use**: Works even without internet connectivity for basic transit fares.

### How to Use

1. **Obtain a Card**: Purchase a new card for approximately 2,500 won (deposit included) or use an existing one.
2. **Reload**: Add funds as needed; minimum reload is often 1,000 won.
3. **Tap**: Simply tap the card on the reader when prompted.
4. **Check Balance**: Can be checked at vending machines, convenience stores, or via the T-money app.
5. **Lost Card**: If you register your card online, you can block it and transfer the balance to a new card.

## Practical Tips for Foreigners

- **Language Settings**: Most vending machines and apps offer English language options.
- **Offline Maps**: Download subway and bus maps in advance for areas with limited internet.
- **Peak Hours**: Avoid rush hours (7:30–9:30 AM and 6:00–8:00 PM) if possible for a more comfortable ride.
- **Safety**: Korea's transit is very safe; however, keep an eye on belongings in crowded areas.
- **Lost Card**: If you register your card online, you can block it and transfer the balance to a new card.
- **Travel with Luggage**: Use elevators or escalators when carrying large bags; avoid blocking doors during peak hours.
- **Night Buses**: Some routes operate late night; check schedules in advance.
- **Airport Rail Express (AREX)**: Direct subway line to Incheon Airport; convenient for travelers with heavy luggage.
- **Station Amenities**: Many subway stations offer convenience stores, ATMs, and restrooms, making transfers more convenient.
- **Environmental Impact**: Using public transit reduces carbon footprint and helps combat urban air pollution, making it an eco-friendly choice for exploring Korea.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use a foreign credit card to pay for transit?**  
A: Most transit readers only accept T-money, cash, or Korean bank cards. Foreign credit cards are generally not accepted at turnstiles or bus readers. It is recommended to obtain a T-money card.

**Q: Do I need to tap out when transferring between subway lines?**  
A: No, tapping out is only required when exiting the subway system or when transferring to a bus that requires tap-out. For subway-to-subway transfers, simply stay within the paid area and follow signs to your next line.

**Q: Are there discounts for students or children?**  
A: Yes, children under 6 ride free. Elementary, middle, and high school students with a valid student ID card receive discounted fares. Foreigners with an alien registration card may also qualify for certain discounts; check with local transit offices.

**Q: How do I find the correct bus stop for my destination?**  
A: Use apps like KakaoMap, Naver Map, or Seoul Bus to search for your destination and see which bus numbers stop nearby. Bus stop signs also list routes and destinations.

**Q: Is it rude to eat or drink on public transportation?**  
A: Eating and drinking are generally discouraged on subways and buses to maintain cleanliness. Some long-distance buses may allow it, but it is best to avoid unless explicitly permitted.

**Q: What if I lose my T-money card?**  
A: If you have registered your card online via the T-money website, you can report it lost and transfer the remaining balance to a new card. Unregistered cards cannot be recovered, so registration is recommended for long-term stays.

## Conclusion

Mastering Korea's public transportation opens up the country for exploration and daily convenience. By understanding how to use the subway, bus, and T-money card, foreigners can travel efficiently, save money, and experience Korea like a local.
