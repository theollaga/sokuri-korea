## What "Sokuri" Means

**Sokuri (소쿠리)** is the Korean word for a woven basket — a humble, everyday object that has gathered Korean ingredients, stories, and daily life for generations. In that same spirit, this blog is a basket where we collect and share Korea with the world, one guide at a time.

## Our Purpose

Sokuri Korea exists to bridge the gap between Korea and the rest of the world. While interest in Korean culture is growing globally, reliable, easy-to-understand information in English can still be scattered or overly academic. Our goal is to provide clear, accurate, and practical guides that help international readers truly engage with Korea — whether they are preparing for a visit, starting a new life here, or simply satisfying their curiosity from afar.

## Who We Serve

Our primary audience includes:

- Expats and newcomers settling in Korea
- Short-term visitors and tourists
- Students and researchers of Korean studies
- Professionals working with Korean partners
- Anyone worldwide with a genuine interest in Korean life

We write with the assumption that readers may be unfamiliar with Korean customs, language, or geography, so we explain concepts thoroughly without assuming prior knowledge.

## What We Cover

Sokuri Korea organizes content into six core sections:

**Food & Drink**
Korean cuisine, dining etiquette, street food, cafés, and drinking culture.

**Travel**
Destination guides, transportation, accommodation tips, seasonal events, and practical advice for traveling across Korea.

**Culture & Lifestyle**
Traditions, holidays, social norms, everyday practices, and modern Korean society.

**Language**
Hangul basics, essential phrases, pronunciation, grammar fundamentals, and resources for learners.

**K-Entertainment**
K-Pop, K-Drama, Korean films, webtoons, and the broader Korean entertainment industry.

**Living in Korea**
Practical guides for expats: visa, housing, banking, healthcare, jobs, and daily life.

## Our Approach

All content is researched carefully and written with an international reader in mind. We strive to:

- Use clear, conversational English
- Provide context for cultural references
- Highlight practical tips you can apply immediately
- Cite reliable sources where appropriate
- Avoid stereotypes and present a balanced view

While we aim for accuracy, we encourage readers to verify critical information (such as visa requirements or transportation schedules) with official sources, as details can change over time.
